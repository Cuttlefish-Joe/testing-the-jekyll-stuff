I'm at the final stage of my HK RGC-funded PhD Fellowship at City University of Hong Kong. My PhD project investigates the politics of web-maps through controversies, with a focus on Jerusalem. More broadly, my research interests include the politics of digital technologies, postcolonial geographies, and political participation.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a mapping assistant to Grassroots Jerusalem, a Palestinian NGO that, in addition and support to their advocacy work, collaborates with local communities to map Jerusalem from a Palestinian perspective.
